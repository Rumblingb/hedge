# Bill Profit Playbook

## Goal

Give Bill the best chance to become profitable by copying the parts of systematic hedge-fund practice that fit the current runtime, while refusing to fake readiness.

## What To Copy

1. Diversified trend, not single-market hero trades.
Bill should keep building around cross-asset trend and managed-futures-style research because the durable edge there comes from many markets, both long and short, with strict sizing and diversification.

2. Carry as a context layer, not blind leverage.
Carry is a useful cross-asset prior for futures and macro positioning, but it should be treated as a regime and ranking input first, not an always-on standalone lever.

3. Relative value and market neutrality before more outright beta.
The next strong research wedge after prediction and current futures work is relative value: spread logic, market-neutral ranking, curve/correlation/dispersion ideas, and better comparison across similar contracts.

4. Market-making discipline for prediction lanes.
Prediction profit will not come from hand-wavy “AI picks.” It will come from fillability-aware quoting, fee-aware routing, inventory discipline, and identifying when a spread is real enough to survive costs.

5. Event-driven and macro scheduling.
Bill should keep tying futures and prediction decisions to known catalysts like CPI, Fed, and rates releases because these are real state transitions, not narrative noise.

## What Not To Copy

1. No leverage escalation to manufacture returns.
2. No weakening of out-of-sample or drawdown gates just to produce a green board.
3. No pretending market-neutral or dispersion is live before there is an actual venue path and execution model.
4. No overfitting to a single recent regime or one “hot” symbol.

## Immediate Research Priorities

1. Trend following across futures and macro instruments.
2. Carry and term-structure signals that can improve ranking and regime labels.
3. Execution alpha, market making, and inventory-risk material for prediction and options.
4. Market-neutral and relative-value research for the next non-directional lane.
5. Options volatility/dispersion material as a research-only build queue, not a fake live lane.

## Immediate Product Implications

1. Prediction stays execution-first but only when net edge survives fees and liquidity.
2. Futures stays gated until OOS stability and tail-risk improve.
3. Researcher should bias toward official strategy and market-structure sources, not only academic PDFs.
4. Hermes should keep telling the truth when the blocker is economics, not infrastructure.
